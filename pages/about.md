## About the Collection

The videos were recorded over a period that spans approximately from the late 1960s to the
first half of the 1980s, and they document family memories: travels, ceremonies (weddings,
christenings, …), family gatherings, and moments of daily life. The videotapes have been
recorded using a camera (model not identified) and stored in shoeboxes. The individual who
started the recording habit and the collection was Giorgio Grammaroli. After the camera
was passed to his first son, Claudio Grammaroli, the collection was initially divided into
two sections. The recordings produced by Giorgio Grammaroli were first stored in his house
and then passed to his last daughter, Simonetta Grammaroli. The recordings produced by
Claudio Grammaroli were stored in his house. In preparation for digitizing the recordings and
making them more easily accessible to the family (the projector used in the past is no longer
available), the two sections of the collection have been reunified. On a first assessment of the
recordings, they appeared to be in well condition of conservation. Each tape is enclosed in a
plastic reel cover and the tapes do not present visuals or other perceptive signals of chemical
deterioration. The collection does not have any form of description and rarely the reels present
tags (most of them are completely discolored) and on a campion analysis some of the tag still
visible may not coincide with the real content of the reel. The collection document the life
of a working class family in the 60s, 70s and the 80s and offer a visual panoramic of the two
neighbors of the city of Rome (Italy) in which the family lived during that time: Fiumicino
(nowadays an independent city, but a municipality of Rome at the time) and Ostia Lido (still a
municipality of the city of Rome). The family life is represented through a mix of daily life and
family events (ceremonies, travels, gatherings).

## Biographical Note

Giorgio Grammaroli is born in 1928 in the Rome neighborhood of San Lorenzo. His family had
to move from their origin home after loosing it in the 1943 bombings (WW2)of San Lorenzo.
The family has moved in several project houses over several zones of the city before settling
a first time in the coastal area of Fiumicino at the end of the 1950s. Here Giorgio and his
son Claudio (born in the 1951) have worked in a naval shipyard under a US company (Chris
Crafts). The family finally moved in the 1968 in the near coastal neighborhood of Ostia Lido.
In 1973, Claudio Grammaroli married Maddalena Cuccureddu and settle with his new family
in the same area. He left the occupation at the shipyard to work in a bank.

## Arrangement

The collection is arranged in two series. The first serie contains the video matierials recorded
by Giorgio Grammaroli and Claudio Grammaroli in Fiumicino and Ostia Lido (including
some travels' recordings) from the 1960s to the 1980s with memory of the family of Giorgio
(Giorgio, his wife Liliana Sassetti and his three children Claudio, Marina and Simonetta. The
first serie contains the video materials recorded by Claudio Grammaroli from the 1970s to the
1980, since he married and settle his own family (Claudio, his wife Maddalena Cuccureddu
and his son Enrico).
